Rivers and Lakes

Discover India's most captivating rivers and lakes, offering serene beauty, cultural significance, and natural splendor.

Ganga River

Known as the lifeline of India, the Ganges holds immense cultural and spiritual significance and flows through northern plains, supporting millions.

Dal Lake

A gem of Srinagar, Dal Lake is surrounded by snow-capped mountains and lush gardens, attracting visitors worldwide.

Brahmaputra River

Originating from Tibet, the Brahmaputra is revered for its power and biodiversity, nurturing Assam's vibrant ecosystems.

Chilika Lake

Known for its scenic backwaters and tranquil, less crowded beaches.

Krishna

Flowing through the southern states, the Krishna River supports irrigation and sustains livelihoods in its vast basin.

Pushkar Lake

Surrounded by temples, this sacred lake is associated with the Pushkar Camel Fair and is a center of devotion and tourism.

Yamuna River

Flowing alongside the Taj Mahal, the Yamuna blends natural beauty with cultural and historical significance.

Loktak Lake

India's largest freshwater lake, Loktak is home to unique ecosystems and the Keibul Lamjao National Park.

Godavari River

A cultural hotspot with stunning sunrises and local handicrafts.

Vembanad Lake

Nestled in the backwaters of Kerala, Vembanad offers tranquil houseboat rides and breathtaking views.

Narmada River

Flowing through central India, the Narmada is celebrated for its scenic beauty, with attractions like the marble rocks of Bhedaghat and spiritual significance.

Pangtong Lake

Famous for its ever-changing hues, Pangong Tso lies at an altitude of 4,350 meters, attracting adventurers and nature lovers worldwide.
