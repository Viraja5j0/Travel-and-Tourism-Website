Forest Destinations

Discover India’s lush forest destinations, offering serene landscapes, diverse wildlife, and a connection to nature.

Sundarban,West Bengal

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Sundarbans is a vast network of tidal waterways, mudflats, and small islands, rich in flora and fauna.

Highlights: Largest mangrove forest and home to the Royal Bengal Tiger.

Jim-corbett,Uttarakhand

Nestled in the Himalayan foothills, this park offers breathtaking views, wildlife safaris, and a chance to explore dense forests.

Highlights: Oldest national park in India, renowned for its tiger population.

Kaziranga,Assam

Spread across floodplains of the Brahmaputra River, it is a biodiversity hotspot featuring elephants, tigers, and migratory birds.

Highlights: Habitat of the one-horned rhinoceros.

Gir Forest,Gujarat

A dry deciduous forest with a unique ecosystem supporting a wide range of species, including leopards and hyenas.

Highlights: Last refuge of the Asiatic lion.

Periyar,Kerela

Centered around the Periyar Lake, this sanctuary provides a lush green retreat and the thrill of spotting wildlife.

Highlights: Famous for its spice plantations and elephant safaris.

Bandipur,Karnataka

This dry deciduous forest is a haven for tigers, elephants, and a variety of bird species.

Highlights: A vital part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve.

Namdapha,ArunachalPradesh

Encompassing diverse ecosystems, from tropical rainforests to alpine vegetation, this park is a treasure trove for nature lovers.

Highlights: One of India’s richest areas in terms of biodiversity

Satpura,Madhyapradesh

Offers a peaceful escape with jeep safaris, nature walks, and a chance to spot leopards and sloth bears.

Highlights: A unique blend of rugged terrain and rich biodiversity.

Andaman and Nicobar Rain Forests

Known for their untouched beauty, these forests provide a lush, green paradise surrounded by pristine beaches.

Highlights: Dense tropical forests with exotic flora and fauna.
