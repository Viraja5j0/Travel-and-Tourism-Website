Polar-like regions

Explore India's mesmerizing polar-like regions, where breathtaking icy landscapes, unique wildlife, and extreme conditions await.

Leh Ladakh

Leh, located in the high-altitude region of Ladakh, experiences freezing temperatures, especially in winter. The terrain here is barren and harsh, similar to some polar regions.

Spiti

Known for its arid, cold desert landscape, Spiti Valley has some of the most extreme weather conditions in India, especially in winter when temperatures can drop well below freezing.

Nubra Valley

Situated at the confluence of the Shyok and Nubra rivers, Nubra Valley experiences cold desert weather, and its winter temperatures can be extremely low.

Kargil

Kargil is another cold region in Jammu & Kashmir, located at high altitudes with snow-covered terrain and freezing temperatures in winter.

Drass

Drass is one of the coldest inhabited places in India, often referred to as the "Gateway to Ladakh." Winter temperatures can plummet to -40°C.

Zanskar

Zanskar is a remote valley known for its icy landscapes and frigid climate. The area is largely inaccessible in winter due to heavy snowfall and freezing temperatures.

Tawar

Located in the northeastern part of India, Tawang is a high-altitude town surrounded by snow-covered mountains, offering harsh, cold climates during winter months.

Siachen

As one of the highest battlefields in the world, Siachen Glacier is located at an altitude of over 6,000 meters, experiencing some of the coldest temperatures in India.

Manali

While not as extreme as some of the other regions, Manali, located in the Kullu Valley, experiences cold, snowy winters with temperatures often dipping below freezing.

Hemis

Located in the cold desert region of Ladakh, Hemis National Park is one of the highest national parks in the world. It’s home to rare wildlife and experiences extreme temperatures in the winter.
