Hill Stations

Discover the best hill stations in India for an unforgettable holiday. Experience serene and scenic destinations that will make your trip truly memorable.

Kulu Valley

Kullu Valley is a broad open valley in Himachal Pradesh, India, formed by the Beas River .
The Valley's majestic hills covered with pine and deodar forest and sprawling apple orchards.
It is sandwiched between the Pir Panjal, Lower Himalayan and Great Himalayan Ranges.


Mussoorie

Mussoorie, also known as Queen of the Hills, is Located on a 15-km-long horseshoe ridge with the grand Himalayas as a backdrop, Mussoorie spreads across at a height of 2,000 m above sea level. From this vantage point, it offers scenic views of Himalayas peaks in Western Garhwal.

Auli

The small yet picture-perfect town of Auli is India's premier ski resort destination. Originally developed as a paramilitary base, Auli's skiing slopes are popular among tourists and professionals alike.For skiing enthusiasts, January to March is the best time to visit.

Munnar

Munnar rises as three mountain streams merge - Mudrapuzha, Nallathanni and Kundala,1,600 m above sea level. One of the most sought after honeymoon destinations in Kerala, Munnar is replete with resorts and lodging facilities that fit a wide rage of budgets. Sprawling tea plantations, picturesque towns, winding lanes and holiday facilities make this a popular resort town.

Gulmarg

Gulmarg literally means “Meadow of flowers”. Gulmarg is located 60 KMs away from Srinagar. The old name of Gulmarg was “Gaurimarg”, the name of Lord Shiva’s wife. King Yousuf Shah Chak changed its name to Gulmarg, meaning the place of roses.

Coorg

Coorg, officially known as Kodagu, is the most affluent hill station in Karnataka. It is well known for its breathtakingly exotic scenery and lush greenery. Forest covered hills, spice and coffee plantations only add to the landscape. Madikeri is the region's centre point with all transportation for getting around starting from here.
