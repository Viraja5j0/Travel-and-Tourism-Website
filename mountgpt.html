Leh-Ladakh, Jammu & Kashmir

Known for its stunning landscapes, monasteries, and vibrant culture, Leh-Ladakh is a paradise for adventure seekers and nature lovers.

Highlights: Pangong Lake, Nubra Valley, Magnetic Hill

Shimla, Himachal Pradesh

The Queen of Hills, Shimla offers colonial architecture, scenic beauty, and a pleasant climate, making it a favorite summer retreat.

Highlights: Mall Road, Ridge, Jakhoo Temple

Manali, Himachal Pradesh

A hub for adventure sports, Manali is famous for its beautiful valleys, hot springs, and snow-capped peaks.

Highlights: Solang Valley, Rohtang Pass, Hadimba Temple

Dharamshala, Himachal Pradesh

The residence of the Dalai Lama, Dharamshala is known for its serene beauty and Tibetan culture.

Highlights: McLeod Ganj, Bhagsu Waterfall, Dharamshala Cricket Stadium

Kasol, Himachal Pradesh

Known for its scenic beauty and hippie culture, Kasol is a small village on the banks of the Parvati River.

Highlights: Parvati Valley, Tosh, Manikaran Sahib

Spiti Valley, Himachal Pradesh

Spiti Valley, a cold desert mountain valley, offers rugged landscapes, ancient monasteries, and unique cultural experiences.

Highlights: Key Monastery, Chandratal Lake, Kaza

Mussoorie, Uttarakhand

Mussoorie, the Queen of the Hills, is known for its colonial heritage, pleasant weather, and stunning views.

Highlights: Kempty Falls, Mall Road, Gun Hill

Nainital, Uttarakhand

Nainital is famous for its beautiful lake, pleasant weather, and scenic landscapes.

Highlights: Naini Lake, Naina Devi Temple, Snow View Point

Auli, Uttarakhand

Auli is known for its skiing resorts, stunning views of the Himalayas, and beautiful meadows.

Highlights: Auli Ropeway, Artificial Lake, Gurso Bugyal

Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

Known as the Yoga Capital of the World, Rishikesh is famous for its spiritual atmosphere and adventure sports.

Highlights: Laxman Jhula, Triveni Ghat, River Rafting

Munnar, Kerala

Munnar is known for its tea gardens, lush greenery, and tranquil atmosphere.

Highlights: Eravikulam National Park, Mattupetty Dam, Tea Plantations

Coorg, Karnataka

Known as the Scotland of India, Coorg is famous for its coffee plantations, scenic beauty, and pleasant weather.

Highlights: Abbey Falls, Raja's Seat, Dubare Elephant Camp

Ooty, Tamil Nadu

Ooty is a popular hill station known for its tea gardens, serene landscapes, and colonial architecture.

Highlights: Ooty Lake, Botanical Gardens, Doddabetta Peak

Kodaikanal, Tamil Nadu

Kodaikanal is known for its mist-covered cliffs, beautiful lakes, and lush forests.

Highlights: Kodai Lake, Coaker's Walk, Pillar Rocks

Mount Abu, Rajasthan

The only hill station in Rajasthan, Mount Abu is known for its cool climate and Dilwara Temples.

Highlights: Nakki Lake, Dilwara Temples, Guru Shikhar

Gangtok, Sikkim

Gangtok, the capital of Sikkim, is known for its Buddhist monasteries, vibrant culture, and stunning landscapes.

Highlights: MG Marg, Rumtek Monastery, Tsomgo Lake

Pelling, Sikkim

Pelling is known for its stunning views of the Kanchenjunga range and rich history.

Highlights: Pemayangtse Monastery, Rabdentse Ruins, Kanchenjunga Falls

Darjeeling, West Bengal

Darjeeling is known for its tea plantations, breathtaking views of the Himalayas, and colonial charm.

Highlights: Tiger Hill, Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, Batasia Loop

Kalimpong, West Bengal

Kalimpong is known for its pleasant weather, scenic views, and Buddhist monasteries.

Highlights: Deolo Hill, Zang Dhok Palri Phodang, Kalimpong Cactus Nursery

Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh

Tawang is known for its Buddhist monasteries, stunning landscapes, and cultural significance.

Highlights: Tawang Monastery, Sela Pass, Madhuri Lake

Ziro Valley, Arunachal Pradesh

Ziro Valley is known for its lush green landscapes, vibrant culture, and music festivals.

Highlights: Talley Valley, Ziro Music Festival, Meghna Cave Temple
